Education

Know TB.

The facts that every student, patient, and community member should understand.

01 · What is TB
Tuberculosis Basics
Chronic infectious disease caused by Mycobacterium tuberculosis
Primarily targets the lungs (pulmonary TB)
Can spread to bones, brain, and kidneys (extrapulmonary TB)
Preventable. Curable. Still killing millions.
01
02 · Transmission
How TB Spreads
Airborne — spread via cough, sneeze, or speech droplets
Droplet nuclei (<5 microns) remain infectious in air for hours
Requires prolonged exposure — not from casual contact
NOT spread by handshakes, sharing food, or touch
02
03 · Red Flags
Symptoms to Know
Persistent cough lasting >2 weeks — the hallmark symptom
Night sweats — profuse sweating during sleep
Unexplained progressive weight loss
Low-grade fever, fatigue, haemoptysis in advanced cases
03
04 · Prevention
How to Stay Safe
BCG vaccination — protects neonates against severe TB forms
Good ventilation in homes, schools, and workplaces
Respiratory hygiene — cover mouth when coughing
Early screening for at-risk individuals saves lives
04
05 · Treatment
Treatment Protocol
Standard therapy: 6 months of four-drug combination (HRZE)
Isoniazid · Rifampicin · Pyrazinamide · Ethambutol
Incomplete treatment creates drug-resistant TB (MDR/XDR)
DOTS — Directly Observed Therapy — ensures adherence
05
06 · Myths vs Facts
Common Myths Busted
"TB cannot be cured" — FALSE. Curable with proper treatment
"TB is a disease of the past" — FALSE. 10.7M cases in 2024
"TB is hereditary" — FALSE. Caused by bacteria, not genes
"BCG fully protects adults" — FALSE. Partial protection only

Clinical Education

Clinical Case
Scenarios.

Four illustrative cases covering the full spectrum of TB presentation — from classic pulmonary disease to extrapulmonary complications.

CASE SCENARIO 01 Pulmonary TB
Classic Pulmonary TB

A 28-year-old man presents with a chronic cough for 4 weeks, low-grade fever, night sweats, and progressive weight loss. He has recently begun coughing up blood. He lives in a crowded apartment — a known risk factor for transmission.

Diagnosis
Active pulmonary TB caused by Mycobacterium tuberculosis
Key Clue
Chronic cough + haemoptysis + constitutional symptoms
Note
Pericardial effusion may occur as a concurrent TB complication
SCOME EKSUMSA · Educational Case
CASE SCENARIO 02 Latent TB
Latent TB — Asymptomatic

A 22-year-old medical student undergoes routine tuberculosis screening. She reports no symptoms whatsoever. Her Mantoux (TST) test returns positive. Chest X-ray is completely normal with no parenchymal findings.

Diagnosis
Latent TB — bacteria present but contained; patient is not infectious
Key Clue
Positive TST + no symptoms + normal CXR = latent, not active
Risk
Remains at risk for future reactivation — preventive therapy indicated
SCOME EKSUMSA · Educational Case
CASE SCENARIO 03 Extrapulmonary TB
TB Meningitis

A 5-year-old child presents with fever, headache, vomiting, neck stiffness, and altered consciousness. Symptoms developed gradually over 2 weeks — a subacute course atypical of routine bacterial meningitis.

Diagnosis
Extrapulmonary TB — TB Meningitis
Key Clue
Subacute onset over days-weeks — not the abrupt onset of bacterial meningitis
Danger
High morbidity and mortality — requires urgent diagnosis and treatment
SCOME EKSUMSA · Educational Case
CASE SCENARIO 04 Extrapulmonary TB
TB Pericarditis

A 50-year-old man presents with chest pain, shortness of breath, and bilateral leg swelling. Examination reveals muffled heart sounds and evidence of fluid accumulation around the heart.

Diagnosis
Extrapulmonary TB — TB Pericarditis with pericardial effusion
Key Clue
Classic triad: muffled sounds + raised JVP + hypotension = Beck's Triad
Emergency
Cardiac tamponade is a life-threatening complication requiring urgent drainage

Global Tuberculosis Paradigms: Epidemiological Shifts, Therapeutic Innovations & Emerging Research Frontiers

A comprehensive analysis of where TB control stands in 2026 — from post-pandemic recovery and ultra-short drug regimens to AI-driven diagnostics and the socioeconomic inequities that continue to fuel transmission in high-burden regions including Nigeria.

Key Findings
  • Asymptomatic TB transmission accounts for 25–51% of all spread events
  • New 4-month DS-TB regimen (2HPZM/2HPM) now conditionally recommended
  • BPaLM 6-month regimen replaces injections for MDR/RR-TB treatment
  • M72/AS01E vaccine showed 50% efficacy — Phase III trial underway
  • AI models trained on 32,000 Mtb genomes identifying novel drug targets
  • Climate change, malnutrition, and air pollution are amplifying the epidemic
  • Catastrophic costs affect 50% of TB-affected households in high-burden regions
